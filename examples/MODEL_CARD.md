# Adult Census Income Classifier (demo)

Classifies whether a UCI Adult Census record has annual income above $50k.
This is a teaching demo — the Adult dataset has documented label and
selection bias (Ding et al., 2021, *Retiring Adult*) and should not be
used as a model for new credit-decisioning work.

## Ethical considerations

- The training data encodes 1994 US labour-market structure, including its disparities.
- Demographic parity and equalized odds disagree by construction; the fair model in this card optimised demographic parity.
- Sensitive features (`sex`, `race`) were treated as audit factors, not as model inputs to be optimised away.


## Caveats and recommendations

- Adult is a benchmark dataset, not a production-fit dataset. Re-audit on representative data before any real use.
- i.i.d. evaluation; no temporal hold-out.


EquiML standard caveats:

- Fairness metrics on this audit assume i.i.d. train/test data. Audit results do not transfer to deployment under distribution shift.
- Demographic parity and equalized odds are not jointly satisfiable except in trivial cases. The fair model in this card optimised demographic parity; equalized odds is a different criterion.
- Thresholds for "acceptable" disparity are domain- and jurisdiction-specific. The numbers here are evidence; the conformity assessment is a human and legal exercise.
- This card is a snapshot of one audit run. Re-audit when the model, data, or policy changes.
